Lasagna

Back to Home

Description

Ingredients

  • lasagna noodles
  • ground beef
  • olive oil
  • onion
  • garlic
  • crushed tomatoes
  • tomato sauce
  • chicken broth
  • tomato paste
  • basil
  • parsley
  • italian seasoning
  • salt and ground black pepper
  • ricotta cheese
  • egg
  • mozzarella cheese
  • parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Place the lasagna noodles in a large container and cover them with hot water. Let them sit for about 25 minutes until they soften and bend easily. Drain the water and lay the noodles flat. Set them aside while you work on the sauce. I like to start the noodles first so they are ready by the time the sauce finishes simmering
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium high heat.
  3. Add the ground beef and cook until no pink remains.
  4. Break the meat apart with a spoon as it cooks.
  5. Let it get a nice color before adding anything else.
  6. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion to the pot.
  7. Cook until the onion turns soft and smells wonderful. You will notice the kitchen starts to smell really good at this point.
  8. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, chicken broth, and Italian seasoning.
  9. Stir everything together and let it simmer for 20 minutes.
  10. Add the tomato paste, chopped basil, and half of the parsley.
  11. Keep simmering for another 10 minutes while stirring now and then. I find that this slow simmer is what makes the sauce taste rich and balanced. Rushing it leaves the tomatoes tasting sharp.
  12. Add salt and ground black pepper to taste.
  13. Stir in two tablespoons of Parmesan cheese. Turn off the heat and set the pot aside while you prepare the ricotta. Taste the sauce before you move on. This is your chance to adjust the seasoning.
  14. Combine the ricotta cheese and egg in a bowl.
  15. Stir until smooth and creamy. This only takes a minute and makes the ricotta easier to spread between the layers.
  16. Preheat your oven to 350 F. Spread a small amount of meat sauce on the bottom of your baking pan.
  17. Lay noodles over the sauce, then add more meat sauce, dollops of ricotta, and a good handful of mozzarella and Parmesan. Repeat this layering one more time so you have two full layers. I always make sure each layer gets enough sauce so the noodles stay moist while baking.
  18. Arrange the last layer of noodles on top.
  19. Cover with the remaining meat sauce.
  20. Sprinkle the rest of the parsley, mozzarella, and Parmesan generously over the top
  21. Bake for 30 minutes or until the cheese melts and the edges start to bubble. If the top browns too fast, cover the pan loosely with foil for the first half of baking.
  22. Take the lasagna out of the oven.
  23. Let it rest for about 10 minutes before you slice into it. I know it is tempting to cut right away, but waiting helps the layers hold together when you serve
